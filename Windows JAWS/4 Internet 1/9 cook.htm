A Good Cook

Marcel Dupont is a good cook. Everyone loves his food. He works in a French restaurant. The restaurant is called Les Fleurs. Les Fleurs is French. In English, it means "The Flowers."

Today Marcel is making soup in the kitchen. He puts butter, onion, carrots, and celery into a big pot. Then he adds potatoes, chicken, water, and pepper. He cooks the soup for two hours. It smells wonderful.

Pierre is a dishwasher. He works at the restaurant too. Marcel hears Pierre washing pots and pans at the sink. After a few minutes, he sees Pierre.

"Taste this soup, Pierre," says Marcel. "It is fantastic!"

Pierre tastes the soup. He doesn't speak for a moment. Then Pierre says, "It's OK, but it needs salt."

"No," answers Marcel. "It doesn't need salt!"

"Yes it does," says Pierre, "It needs a little salt."

Marcel is angry with Pierre. He says, "I am a chef. You are a dishwasher. You don't know how to make soup."

"OK," answers Pierre. "But I know how to taste soup. And I know that your soup needs salt!"

The End
