Practice Web Form

A lot of websites that you visit will contain forms. Forms are used to submit information to a website or get the right kind of information from a website.
The most important thing to remember about forms is that you must enter forms mode. Most of the time when you land on a form control, JAWS will put you in forms mode automatically. You will know this because you will hear a high pitched beep. Occasionally, however, JAWS will not put you into forms mode automatically and you will have to turn on forms mode. To turn on forms mode, you must hit the spacebar. Really what this means is that in order to enter text in a text field -- such as the name field below, you will need to hit the spacebar. Then JAWS will say "forms mode on". If you don't enter forms mode, JAWS may interpret the keys you type as the navigation commands you learned earlier. This is especially annoying because this can move you to somewhere else on the page and force you to lose your place. Occasionally you will need to exit forms mode and go back to the regular browsing mode so you can read the text surrounding a control. You can do this by hitting escape . You can then use your standard reading commands. To continue filling out the form, you will need to re-enter forms mode.
So practice using your navigation keystrokes--like e for edit box and x for checkbox, etc. Then fil out this form. Don't worry, its just practice.
