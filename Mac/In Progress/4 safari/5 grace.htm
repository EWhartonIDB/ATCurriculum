My Name Is Grace

Grace Warner is a nurse. She lives in Chicago. She works in a big hospital downtown. She likes her job.

Grace is in Room 516. She is talking to Mr. Jones. He is an old man. He is 94. Mr. Jones is in the hospital bed. He has a broken hip.

Mr. Jones asks, "What is your name?"

"My name is Grace," she answers.

"What?" asks Mr. Jones. "I can't hear you."

"My name is Grace," she says again.

"I don't understand. How do you spell your name?" asks Mr. Jones.

"G-R-A-C-E," she answers.

"Oh, Grace! That's a beautiful name!" says Mr. Jones. Then he asks, "Are you married?"

"No," Grace answers. "I am single."

"Good," says Mr. Jones, "I am single too! How old are you, Grace?"

"I am 29," Grace answers.

"I am sorry," says Mr. Jones. "But you are too old for me!"

The End
