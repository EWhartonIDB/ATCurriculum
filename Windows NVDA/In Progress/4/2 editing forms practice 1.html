Practice Web Form

A lot of websites that you visit will contain forms. Forms are used to submit information to a website or get the right kind of information from a website.
The most important thing to remember about forms is that you must enter focus mode. Most of the time when you land on a form control, NVDA will put you in focus mode automatically. You will know this because you will hear a high pitched beep with a click. Occasionally, however, NVDA will not put you into focus mode automatically and you will have to turn on focus mode. To turn on focus mode, can hit caps+spacebar. Really what this means is that in order to enter text in a text field -- such as the name field below, you will need to hit caps+spacebar. Then NVDA will make the beep with a click. If you don't enter focus mode, NVDA may interpret the keys you type as navigation commands This is especially annoying because this can move you to somewhere else on the page and force you to lose your place. Occasionally you will need to exit focus mode and go back to the regular browsing mode so you can read the text surrounding a control. You can do this by hitting escape . You can then use your standard reading commands. To continue filling out the form, you will need to re-enter focus mode.
