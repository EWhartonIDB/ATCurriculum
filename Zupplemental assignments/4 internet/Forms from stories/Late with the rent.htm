Late with the Rent

Every month Vera collects the rent from the tenants in her apartment building. Most of the tenants are very responsible and pay on time.

However, there is one tenant who is always late. It is Miss Walker in apartment 3-B.

Miss Walker has many excuses for not paying her rent on time. One month she says her paycheck is late. The next month she says there is a problem at the bank. "Sorry," Miss Walker says to Vera every month. "It isn't my fault."

Vera feels annoyed by Miss Walker's excuses. Still, Miss Walker is a pleasant person, and she gets along with the other tenants. But after Miss Walker is one week late with the rent, Vera knocks on her door.

"I'm sorry about your problems," says Vera. "But your rent is due on the first day of every month. Today is the seventh. You need to pay it."

"Oh, of course," says Miss Walker. She gets out her checkbook and writes Vera a check for $500.

"Just don't cash this before the tenth," says Miss Walker. "It isn't my fault, but right now there is no money in my checking account."

The End.
