Terrible Food

Chan works in a retirement home and helps take care of the elderly people living there.

Most of the people are very nice, but Mrs. Stevens is always complaining about something. For one thing, she always criticizes the food.

It's never right. The meat is tough. The vegetables are overcooked. The butter is hard and the bread is stale.

One day Mrs. Stevens is eating chicken noodle soup.

"How is it?" asks Chan.

"It's terrible," says Mrs. Stevens. "They can't make chicken noodle soup here. I have a recipe for chicken noodle soup that is much better."

"Can you give me the recipe?" asks Chan. "I will give it to the cook."

Mrs. Stevens writes down the recipe and Chan gives it to the cook.

Chan is very excited. Finally she can give Mrs. Stevens some food she likes.

Next Tuesday, Chan brings Mrs. Stevens a bowl of soup. "I hope you like it," says Chan. "The cook followed your recipe exactly." Mrs. Stevens picks up her spoon and tastes the soup. "

How is it?" asks Chan. "It isn't very good," says Mrs. Stevens. "I never liked that recipe."

The End.
