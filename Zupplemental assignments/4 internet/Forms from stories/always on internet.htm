Always on the Internet

Emma is always on the Internet. She loves the computer, and she shops on the Internet. She buys furniture, clothes, books, and any other things on the Internet.

She also finds a lot of information there. Emma sees all the news and reads interesting articles about health and fitness.

Emma's son Hans is happy that his mother likes the Internet. He is glad that she is not afraid to use the computer anymore.

But he is worried that she spends so much time sitting in front of it. Hans thinks she needs to go out more.

One day Hans walks into the house. "Mom," he asks. "What are you doing?"

"Oh, I'm buying my groceries on the Internet," answers Emma.

"It's a beautiful day," says Hans. "Go to the supermarket. You can see people and walk around. It's good for your health."

"Not today," says Emma. "I need to stay inside. It's going to rain."

"How do you know?" asks Hans. "You're always inside at the computer."

"I know about the weather," says Emma. "I checked the report on the Internet."

The End.
