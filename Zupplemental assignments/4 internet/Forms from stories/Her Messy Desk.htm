Her Messy Desk

Ed's boss, Miss Benson, needs to leave the office for a while. Before she leaves, she tells Ed what to do. Miss Benson says, "Please answer the phone and take messages for me.

Please address these envelopes. Oh, and one more thing. My desk is a mess. I have papers, books, and office supplies everywhere. Maybe you can help me get organized. I'll be back at 2:30."

Miss Benson walks out of the door. Ed walks into Miss Benson's office and looks at her desk. She is right. Her desk is a mess.

Ed sorts through the papers. He files the papers in the filing cabinet. He puts books on the bookshelf. Then Ed puts away the office supplies: stapler, tape, scissors, rubber bands, and paper clips.

At 2:30 Miss Benson returns to the office. Ed gives her the phone messages and the envelopes. Then Miss Benson looks at her desk. It is neat and organized.

"Thank you, Ed," says Miss Benson. "You are a wonderful assistant. Now please get out all of my papers, books, and office supplies. I have a lot of work to do."

The End.
