Project Proposal

As a part of communications class, each student must choose projects to work on outside of class. You will need to complete three small projects and a large final project. These projects should be a braille project or a project that combines braille and technology. Here is a list of potential project ideas:

Braille projects:


  • reading a braille book, certain number of braille volumes or pages of braille
  • creating a braille phone list or address book
  • creating a braille recipe book
  • creating a set of braille flash cards
  • compiling a notebook of computer commands
  • keeping a journal in braille
  • finding a braille pen pal and exchanging letters on a regular basis
  • creating a braille file of important account numbers, passwords, etc.
  • learning grade 3 braille or developing your own braille shorthand
  • building slate writing speed and accuracy by transcribing something from audio to braille
  • creating a binder of song lyrics transcribed into braille
  • labeling your CDs, DvDs, or other collections
  • create a file of favorite quotes or sayings in braille
  • create a set of labels for canned goods
  • prepare a speech or dramatic reading from braille text or note cards
  • braille a deck of playing cards or card game

Combo Projects:


  • read a DAISY book on a NLS player, computer, or portable book player and take notes in braille
  • look up recipes or food directions online and write them out with a slate and stylus
  • read a book in braille and type notes on a computer or braille notetaker
  • scan and edit a restaurant menu to proofread and emboss in braille

These are only suggestions. You may design your own projects, however, the instructor will need to approve them in order to give credit. Please fill out the form below for each project you are undertaking.
